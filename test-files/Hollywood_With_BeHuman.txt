Hollywood is a neighborhood in Los Angeles, California. It is the historical center of the American film industry. Film companies moved to Hollywood in the early 1910s. They were escaping Thomas Edison's patent restrictions on the East Coast. By the 1920s, Hollywood was producing most of the world's movies.

The Hollywood Walk of Fame was established in 1958. It has over 2,700 stars along 15 blocks of Hollywood Boulevard and three blocks of Vine Street. Each star honors someone in film, television, music, radio, or theater. The TCL Chinese Theatre opened in 1927. Its forecourt has celebrity handprints, footprints, and autographs.

The Academy Awards, or Oscars, have been held in Hollywood since 1929. The ceremony is broadcast to over 200 countries. The Dolby Theatre has hosted it since 2002.

Film production moved to other cities in the late 1900s. Hollywood went through a period of decline. New businesses and theaters arrived in the 2000s.

The Hollywood Sign was put up in 1923. It was a real estate ad that read "Hollywoodland." It sits on Mount Lee at 450 feet tall.
